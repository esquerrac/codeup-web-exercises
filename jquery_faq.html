National Parks Frequently Asked Questions

Are National Parks cool?
National Parks are REALLY cool.

How Many National Parks are there?
There are 417 National Parks in the United States! That's more than 1 park for every million people! Wow!

Are there any states without a National Park?
There are 23 states without a National Park. That means those other 27 states are basically just all park.

How much does it cost to go?
That varies from park to park. If you sneak in through the woods it's always free, though.

Which was the first National Park?
Yellowstone National Park, which, I've been told, is nowhere near Oz, somehow.

Which is the most popular National Park?
Great Smoky Mountains park routinely draws the most visitors. That's actually pretty impressive for a park I've never heard of.

Are there any National Parks in an ocean?
88 National Parks include coastal and oceanic regions in their protected domain.

Which National Park is the largest?
Oddly enough, Wrangell is the largest. Another park I had never heard of until I started this class.

Is Mayonnaise a National Park?
No, Patrick. Horeseradish is not a National Park either.

What is a surprising fact about National Parks?
Apparently they're all surprising, because I knew literally none of these answers before starting the exercise.

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

  • GSM Park is the most visited National Park in the United States
  • There are over 100 different species of tree in the park
  • GSM has never recorded a temperature above 80 degrees. Get ready for some nice hiking weather!
  • This National Park doesn't charge admission

Wrangell National Park

  • This is the largest National Park in the United States
  • Wrangell is so large you could fit 6 Yellowstone Parks inside of it!
  • Wrangell National Park contains one of the most profitable copper mines in the United States
  • This park contains 25% of all the glaciers in North America

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone is the oldest National Park in the United States
  • This park actually expands into three States (Wyoming, Montana, Idaho)
  • There are over 300 geysers at Yellowstone
  • Old Faithful, the most famous geyser, erupts every 92 minutes
